IHEC Carthage - Institut des Hautes Études Commerciales de Carthage
====================================================================

Overview:
IHEC Carthage is one of Tunisia's leading public business schools, located in Carthage, Tunis. It was founded in 1942, originally as the "Institut des Hautes Études Commerciales de Tunis," and has since grown into a prestigious institution for business and management education in North Africa.

Programs Offered:
- Licence Fondamentale en Gestion (LFG): A 3-year undergraduate program covering the fundamentals of business management, including accounting, finance, marketing, and management.
- Licence Appliquée en Gestion (LAG): A 3-year applied undergraduate program with specializations such as Finance, Marketing, and Management Information Systems.
- Master de Recherche: A research-oriented master's degree in fields like Finance, Marketing, and Management.
- Master Professionnel: A professionally-oriented master's degree focusing on practical skills for the job market.
- Doctorat en Sciences de Gestion: A doctoral program in management sciences for advanced research.

Departments:
1. Department of Finance and Accounting - Covers corporate finance, financial markets, auditing, and management accounting.
2. Department of Marketing - Focuses on consumer behavior, digital marketing, brand management, and market research.
3. Department of Management and Strategy - Covers organizational behavior, strategic management, human resources, and entrepreneurship.
4. Department of Quantitative Methods and Information Systems - Focuses on statistics, data analysis, business intelligence, and information systems.
5. Department of Economics - Covers microeconomics, macroeconomics, international economics, and economic policy.
6. Department of Law - Provides courses on commercial law, labor law, tax law, and international business law.

Academic Calendar:
- The academic year typically starts in mid-September and ends in late June.
- Fall semester: September to January, with exams in January.
- Spring semester: February to June, with exams in May/June.
- Resit exams (session de rattrapage) are usually held in late June or early July.
- Registration for the new academic year opens in early September.

Admission:
- Admission to the Licence programs is based on the national orientation system after the Baccalauréat exam.
- Master's programs require a Licence degree with competitive selection based on academic performance.
- Doctoral admission requires a Master's degree and a research proposal.

Campus Facilities:
- A modern library with access to academic journals, databases, and study rooms.
- Computer labs equipped with business software (SAP, SPSS, STATA).
- An amphitheater and multiple lecture halls.
- A student club area for extracurricular activities and events.
- Sports facilities including a football field and basketball court.
- A cafeteria offering affordable meals for students and staff.

Student Life:
- IHEC Carthage has a vibrant student community with numerous clubs and associations.
- Major student clubs include: Club IHEC Entrepreneurship, Club Marketing, Club Finance, Club Culturel, Club Sportif, and Junior Entreprise IHEC.
- The school organizes annual events such as the IHEC Business Forum, Marketing Week, and Finance Day.
- Students have opportunities for internships with Tunisian and international companies.
- Exchange programs are available with partner universities in France, Canada, and other countries.

Frequently Asked Questions:
Q: What are the working hours of the administration?
A: The administration office is open Monday to Friday from 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM, and Saturday from 8:30 AM to 1:00 PM.

Q: How can I get my student certificate (attestation de présence)?
A: You can request it at the scolarité office. Bring your student ID card. It usually takes 24 to 48 hours to process.

Q: What is the grading system?
A: IHEC uses a 20-point grading system. A score of 10/20 or above is considered a passing grade. The mention system is: 10-12 Passable, 12-14 Assez Bien, 14-16 Bien, 16+ Très Bien.

Q: Are there scholarships available?
A: Yes, scholarships (bourses) are available for students based on financial need. Applications are submitted through the university social services office (ONOU).

Q: Can I transfer from another university?
A: Transfer requests are evaluated on a case-by-case basis. You need to submit your transcripts and a formal request to the administration during the registration period.

Q: What languages are used for instruction?
A: Most courses are taught in French. Some courses, especially at the Master's level, may be offered in English or Arabic.
